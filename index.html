About Me

I'm a Cognitive Science B.A. (and enthusiast) turned developer.

As an undergrad, my favorite subject was Computational Linguistics. I found it fascinating how one could take something so complex as a language and simplify it into a set of universal states. I had the most fun using logic and grammar to piece together an understanding of unfamiliar languages.

To me, writing programs follows a similar process of decoding languages and discerning how to communicate within them. They share general underlying functions, but differ in presentation; System.out.println() is to print() as "Hello World" is to "Bonjour le monde".

Cheesiness aside, my interest in language technology fuels my career as a developer. I'm eager to see how this field develops and makes communication more universal and accessible. If this also interests you, check out my team's Moonshot Project from our Language and the Brain course! We investigated how we can advance translation technology using facial and emotional recognition data.
